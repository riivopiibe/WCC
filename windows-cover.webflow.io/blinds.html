Blinds

Blinds are not particularly noticeable in the interior and offer a wide range of options, both in terms of material, pattern and color. The selection of blinds includes both textile blinds and cassette blinds, where you can also choose between standard fabric and blackout fabric.

Which blinds to choose?
The most widely used window coverings are textile blinds, which are a practical and good choice, providing effective protection from sunlight. Textile blinds can be installed on the window frame, in the window opening or on the wall, depending on the customer's wishes and needs. More over, in the rich color selection of textile blinds you can find a suitable Â solution for every apartment, whether yellow, purple, green, Â 
white, black or blinds with an interesting pattern. A blind can be a pure window protection, but depending on the color, it can also be a lively furnishing element.

Since sunlight and the need for blinds vary in different rooms, the selection of textile blinds includes transparent, semi-obscuring, sun-reflecting and blackout blinds suitable Â Â for each room according to the situation there. Blinds are also useful to protect privacy: Semi-blackout or blackout blinds help to avoid prying eyes of neighbors or passers-by.

1.Transparent blinds let light through, do not obstruct the view, but on the other hand prevent UV radiation from entering the room.
2.Semi-darkening blinds let light through, you can see shadows and movement, but you cannot see clearly through them.
3.Darkening blinds do not let light through, and if the blinds are measured accurately, no light should shine through from the sides.
4.Sun-reflecting blinds do not let light or heat through, but reflect them back with a shiny back.

Blackout blinds are mostly used in the bedroom, children's room or guest room to ensure undisturbed sleep in the sunny morning hours. In the kitchen, on the other hand, transparent or semi-darkening blinds are a practical choice that brighten the room with natural light but still protect it from direct sunlight. In rooms with a lot of sunlight and resulting heat in summer, it is advisable to use blinds that reflect the sun.

Screens Select screens system

Description of the screen system The Screen is a system combining aesthetics and protection against excessive sunlight and overheating of rooms. It can be used to protect the interiors against the sun without the necessity to avoid using natural lighting .The Screens can be used to reduce the costs of e.g. air conditioning or artificial lighting. Due to increase in energy performance of abuilding we can reduce also running costs. The Screens are ideal both for interiors and when used outside the room. They are ideal for flats, houses, office locations and other public utility buildings. A wide range of fabrics and any colors of the Screen structure gives a great possibility to adapt it to any interior layout. Following the trend of increasing sizes of glazing, we can manufacture the Screens with areas up to 18 m2.

Versions of the screen system

Screen system is available in three installation versions: flush-mounted, top-mounted and surface-mounted (fixed to the facade or against the window clearance).
It consists of four main components: box, bottom bar, material and guides. Three types of material guiding are available:

System ZIP

â€This innovative system of material fixing, which is integrated with the guides due to the use of the Zip closure system. It allows ideal gripping, stretching and guiding of the fabric. ZIP system ensures unique stability and resistance to wind. It can be used also as a insect screen against insects.

System with bottom bar in guides

The material does not move inside the guide, where only the bottom bar is guided. There is free space between the guide and fabric, which is used to ensure good circulation of the air. It is recommended to use in locations covered against strong winds.

System with cable side guides without guides

The fabric is fixed to the bottom bar, which is moving on the metal cables, without the necessity of using the guides.
